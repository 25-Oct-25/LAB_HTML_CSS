The Dawn of AI

The birth of AI began at the Dartmouth Conference (1956). Early AI focused on symbolic reasoning but faced "AI winters" due to limited computing power.

The breakthrough came with Machine Learning and later Deep Learning, powered by big data and GPUs.

Did you know?

The term “AI” was coined by John McCarthy in 1955!

AI's Current Reach

  • Healthcare: Diagnosing diseases and drug discovery.
  • Finance: Fraud detection and algorithmic trading.
  • Education: Personalized learning and tutoring systems.
  • Transportation: Self-driving cars and logistics.
  • Creative Arts: AI-generated art and music.
  • Daily Life: Voice assistants, spam filters, and more.

The Road Ahead

The future of AI is leading toward Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) — systems that can think and learn like humans.

This evolution brings both opportunities and ethical challenges: bias, automation, and aligning AI with human values.
